# Great tone starts with timing

Technique, gear, and tone knobs matter. But the thing players forget first is rhythm discipline.

If your attack is rushed, delayed, or inconsistent, the listener never hears the tone as clearly.

Here is a simple one-week training loop:

* Set a metronome at a moderate tempo
* Record two takes of one 8-bar progression
* Compare swing and subdivision consistency
* Repeat only the section that drifts

Keep the loop simple and consistent. If your rhythm is solid, tone gains definition.

Next, experiment with one sound variable only.

* Pick attack
* Amp gain
* Pick angle
* String attack point

If you change everything at once, you are guessing at what improved your tone.

The most useful practice rule is:

* one note
* one tempo
* three minutes
* focused correction
